Solution iterations for client discovery

solution 1

After comparing my solution to the model work, I have realized that my approach was more instructional rather than guiding. As a person with a technical computer science background, I tackled the problem from a programming perspective, thinking more about high-level details of the implementation. However, I have realized that my solution should provide guidance for people who will not necessary have a technical background. In order to improve my solution, I will need to include more information about basic implementation principles rather than going into details about specific technology choices.

final solution

For the second iteration of the client discovery task, I focused on providing more information for people without technical background. I added a section about Technology Delivery, which includes such factors as Componentization of the solution, Dependency on external factors or solutions, and Timeline for project implementation.

Design a Business Case

As a second task, I needed to design a business case for the client. A business case is a justification for a proposed project on the basis of its expected commercial benefit. My goal was to convince MyBank to proceed with developing of an online banking platform.

  • skills
  • Feasibility Assessment

Self-reflection: Business Case

benefits

After reflecting on my work, I found a few differences and similarities between my business case and the model solution. While my work gave an overview of benefits, feasibility of the project and comparative value analysis, the model solution focused on the different areas of business that will benefit from the new online banking system. Both of the solutions outline similar benefits such as new market opportunities, reduced costs, and convenient automated processes.

costs

As for the cost analysis, I only provided an overview of the costs associated with the system implementation. However, the model solution focuses on the costs that occur when banks adopt online-first approach to their businesses. I believe that both of these types of costs should be accounted for when attempting to implement an online banking system.
